About Me

Like a very young child and a very old woman mixed together, I am BabyElderly.

" 'It is a sign of aphuia' says he,–that is, of a nature not finely tempered,–'to give yourselves up to things which relate to the body; to make, for instance, a great fuss about exercise, a great fuss about eating, a great fuss about drinking, a great fuss about walking, a great fuss about riding. All these things ought to be done merely by the way: the formation of the spirit and character must be our real concern.' This is admirable; and, indeed, the Greek words aphuia, euphuia,+ a finely tempered nature, a coarsely tempered nature, give exactly the notion of perfection as culture brings us to conceive of it: a perfection in which the characters of beauty and intelligence are both present..."

- Matthew Arnold quoting Epictetus, Culture and Anarchy

Past

I was a non-traditional educator, creating and facilitating public programs, tours, and summer camps for ages 4-18 at Children's Theater of Madison Summer Drama School, Wisconsin Historical Museum, and Madison Children's Museum.

I've also supervised visitor services and coordinated events at McCormick Tribune Freedom Museum, The Art Insitute of Chicago, and Madison Children's Museum.
